A Short Hitory

Yosemite Valley, nestled in the Sierra Nevada mountains of California, was formed millions of years ago through a combination of geological processes, including glaciation, which carved out its iconic cliffs and waterfalls. The Ahwahneechee people, a Native American tribe, lived in the valley for centuries before European settlers arrived. In 1851, the Mariposa Battalion, a group of European-American explorers, were the first non-Native Americans to document the valley, leading to increased interest and tourism. Yosemite Valley was officially protected as a state park in 1864, thanks to the efforts of naturalist John Muir and others, eventually becoming a cornerstone of the U.S. National Park System when Yosemite National Park was established in 1890.
